# Product Claims and Next Decision

## 1. Purpose of this document

This document translates the validated case campaign into product-level interpretation.

It is meant to answer three practical questions:

- what Agro-DO can already claim credibly,
- what it should not claim yet,
- and whether it is worth opening another validation family now.

---

## 2. What Agro-DO can already claim credibly

## 2.1 Governed differentiated decision support
Agro-DO already shows that it does not produce one generic response for every severe condition. It differentiates between backup switching, operational adjustment, interruption, and human escalation.

## 2.2 Continuity reasoning under constraint
Agro-DO can reason about whether continuity remains viable, becomes constrained, or should be interrupted.

## 2.3 Internal prioritization under scarcity
Agro-DO can allocate scarce continuity selectively, protecting higher-priority sectors before lower-priority ones.

## 2.4 Recognition of collapse boundaries
Agro-DO can recognize when even the last continuity strategy is no longer credible.

## 2.5 External recovery-path awareness
Agro-DO can change its recommendation when an external backup path exists.

## 2.6 Backup-path credibility calibration
Agro-DO distinguishes between:
- strong clean backup,
- weak or delayed backup,
- and constrained but viable backup.

That is one of the strongest current product claims.

---

## 3. What Agro-DO should not claim yet

## 3.1 It should not claim industrial proof
The current evidence is strong for controlled validation, but it is not industrial field validation.

## 3.2 It should not claim full economic optimization
The system reasons about priority and recovery, but not yet as a full economic optimizer.

## 3.3 It should not claim autonomous closed-loop control
The service still works as governed decision support with human review, not as autonomous end-to-end control.

## 3.4 It should not claim broad long-term planning intelligence
The validated set is mostly about operational decision episodes, not long-horizon adaptive planning.

---

## 4. Product interpretation by family

## 4.1 Scarcity plus hydraulics family
Product meaning:
Agro-DO can detect when scarcity still supports mitigation and when it should cross into interruption.

## 4.2 Internal prioritization family
Product meaning:
Agro-DO can allocate scarce continuity according to priority instead of reacting with a single blunt rule.

## 4.3 External recovery family
Product meaning:
Agro-DO can reason about whether a recovery path actually changes the decision, rather than treating all backups as equal.

---

## 5. Is another family worth opening now?

My answer is: **not by default**.

Another family is only worth opening if it changes the product understanding in a clearly new way.

A new family would be justified only if it tests something materially different, for example:
- conflict between water recovery and plant disease risk,
- competition between recovery and equipment integrity,
- or a multi-constraint scenario where one recovery path creates a second operational risk.

If the next idea is only a nearby variation of families already mapped, it is probably not worth it now.

---

## 6. Recommended decision

The best decision now is:

1. stop the case-generation campaign temporarily,
2. consolidate the claims already supported,
3. and only then decide whether one final genuinely new family adds enough value.

That is the highest-return move.

---

## 7. Practical next step

The practical next step should be to use the validated behavior review and this document to decide one of these two paths:

### Path A — Stop and consolidate
Use the current validated set as the evidence base for:
- product narrative,
- partner communication,
- technical handoff,
- and next-stage planning.

### Path B — Open one final truly new family
Do this only if the project manager identifies a family that clearly changes product understanding instead of extending already-mapped logic.

---

## 8. Final recommendation

My recommendation is **Path A**.

The current campaign is already strong enough to support serious product claims, and the risk of continuing immediately is not under-validation. The risk is dilution through diminishing-return cases.

The right move now is to consolidate what Agro-DO already proves.
